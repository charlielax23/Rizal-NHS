About Us

History Of RNHS

Rizal National High School was established in 1968 through the efforts of community leaders. Over the years, it has seen significant growth in enrollment, facilities and achievements, including winning academic and non-academic contest at both local and national levels. The school underwent conversion to a national high school in 1983 and relocated to a larger campus in 1988. Despite challenges, it celebrated its 50th founding anniversary with a renewed commitment to excellence and community engagement. Currently, the school continues to strive for excellence, aiming to excel nationally while providing accessible and quality education to its students.

Vision

The school's vision is to cultivate a community of empowered learners who embrace knowledge, critical thinking, and ethical values, aspiring to become global citizens.

Mission

Our mission is to provide a dynamic and inclusive learning environment that fosters academic excellence, promotes innovation, and nurtures personal growth.

Aim

We aim to equip students with the skills and mindset needed to navigate a rapidly changing world, instilling a passion for lifelong learning and a commitment to making positive contributions to society.

Achievements

2024 Division Schools Press Conference

The Rizalian National High School journalism team competed in the 2024 Division Schools Press Conference, showcasing their skills in reporting, writing, and editing. Individual and group events included news writing, editorial writing, photojournalism, radio and TV broadcasting, online publishing, collaborative writing, and more. Several participants received awards in various categories, with notable achievements such as 2nd place in news writing for Danielle Angela A. Rubino and 2nd place in editorial writing for Freyanne Zyrelle Pader. Additionally, the team secured 2nd place in radio broadcasting (Filipino) and TV broadcasting (English).

Fire Prevention Month's Poster Making Contest

On March 6, 2024, at the "Fire Prevention Month's Poster Making Contest" organized by the Bureau of Fire Protection - Santiago City Station at Xentro Mall, Villasis, City of Santiago, two high school students showcased their talents. Prince Rhustan S. Casiano, a Grade 11 STEM student, and John Angelo C. Solmayor from Grade 10 Rizal, participated in the event with the theme "Sa Pag-iwas sa Sunog, Hindi ka Nag-iisa". Prince won 1st Place in the Senior High Category, while John Angelo received a Consolation Award in the Junior High Category. Their artwork exemplified the commitment of RNHS School to fire prevention awareness.

AMA Brains Olympiad 2024

On December 1, 2023, the competition inaugurated the National Eliminations for a General Quiz Bee online, with the participation of Prince Rhustan Casiano (Grade 11-STEM), Charles Steven Agraan (Grade 12-STEM), Peter Louie Dagami (Grade 12-STEM), and Ralph Raven Sardoma (Grade 12-ABM), clinching 2nd place among over 455 competing schools nationwide. The quiz bee participants maintained their momentum, advancing to the Semi-Finals after clinching 1st place out of 52 competing schools during the Quarter Finals. Once again, they defended their 1st place in the Semi-Finals, aiming to win the 1st AMA National Brains Olympiad as they competed in Manila. On February 29, 2024, the Finals were held in Manila with 11 competitors. They failed to reach the top 5, but they showcased their brilliance and intellectual prowess in different learning areas. Despite not winning the competetion, Despite not winning the competetion, they should still be acknowledged for their contributions as they made it to the National Finals, proudly representing and promoting the name of Rizal National High School. Their efforts have not only put the school to on the map in Santiago City, but also became known nationwide for their brilliance and intelligence.
